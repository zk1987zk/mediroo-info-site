About MediRoo

Many Australians with chronic diseases have trouble remembering to take their medications on time or deviate from their doctor’s orders. This is often because it can get very hard to keep track of multiple medication regimens, and for chronic diseases it can be tempting to stop taking medications when symptoms seem to go away. Although apps exist to help patients with these issues, they have problems with maintaining user engagement in the long-term.

MediRoo is a mobile application that helps users keep track of their medications with regular reminders, motivating feedback, and a simple interface. The app targets users with multiple daily prescriptions, where it can be difficult to maintain a constant schedule with pills that may be important to health. The app is designed with older patients in mind, who may be less technically inclined and are more easily intimidated by screens with too many points of interaction. This design philosophy is evidenced through the process of adding medications, the notifications the user receives as reminders, the tracking of symptoms and the reporting of the patient's “performance” (in taking pills on time) over time.

The app allows patients to select all the medications they are taking, after which it will remind them to take their medication at the appropriate times, also noting how they are feeling on each occasion. For example, suppose an elderly patient has to keep up with four different prescriptions at varying schedules in the same day. With MediRoo, the patient is able to follow their prescribed medication regimen just by responding to a smartphone’s alerts, resulting in a much lower cognitive load and fewer mistakes. This is just one example of the benefits to users of this app: they would be more likely to experience the benefits of medication when taking it according to the doctor’s instructions, and by using the app they will be more willing and able to stick to their prescribed regimens.
